Projects


University Management System

The project involves the development of a mobile application for a university management system, using the Flutter framework. Back-end has been done using MongoDB and Nodejs. The app will provide a range of features for students, including attendance, class schedules, coursework management, mess timings, library book issues, and much more. The goal is to create a comprehensive, integrated system for all of a student's college needs. I contributed to both the front-end development and the integration with the backend API.

DigiDuniya

DigiDuniya is a platform for digital literacy that aims to address the gap in the Indian market where many individuals are unaware of how to effectively use basic digital tools such as Gmail, WhatsApp, web browsing, and much more. To address this issue, we developed an application comprising of interactive exercises and videos that allow users to learn as well as test their skills and improve their understanding of these tools. For example, our in-app email client simulator allows users to practice sending emails by teaching them how to address and write the subject and content of an email.

Platform for Public Healthcare System

As part of my course, I worked with Dr. Rudra Pratap, the founding VC of Plaksha University, to build a platform to locate Primary Healthcare Centers in India. Right now, there is no platform dedicated to government hospitals. Our goal is to develop a full-fledged Public Healthcare Platform in collaboration with the government, with the aim of providing comprehensive care for every citizen of India. This platform will provide information on the timings and services offered at these centers and will come with features where you can enter your Pincode and get to the nearest centers in your locality and much more.

Analysis of HealthCare System

We analyzed the healthcare system in India using computational thinking techniques to gain a deeper understanding of the healthcare system in India and identify areas that may be in need of improvement. We collected data on the public healthcare system and population for the years 2005 and 2019 from government websites, and then analyzed this data to provide insights into the availability and accessibility of healthcare resources in a given region. The analysis included an examination of various ratios related to healthcare resources, such as the ratio of population to health centers and the ratio of doctors to population.

Quantum Edge Detection

In this project, the performance of classical and quantum edge detection algorithms was compared. A simulation was developed on IBM Quantum Lab to test the quantum edge detection algorithm (QHED) on a 256x256-pixel image, and it was found that the QHED algorithm was able to detect edges in constant time. The QHED algorithm was also found to be more accurate and faster than classical algorithms such as the Canny edge detector and the Sobel operator. The results of this project suggest that quantum algorithms have the potential to improve the performance of image processing tasks, particularly for edge detection.

Localization of Robot

In this project, a discrete Bayesian filter was implemented on MATLAB and used to develop a path planning and localization system for an autonomous ground vehicle (UGV). The UGV was connected to an Arduino board to allow the algorithm to be tested in a real-life simulation. The implemented discrete Bayesian filter was tested on the UGV in a simulated environment and was able to accurately estimate the vehicle's position and orientation as it moved through the environment. For the color detection part, we wrote an algorithm an connected the camera with Arduino to automatically click pictures and detect the color.
